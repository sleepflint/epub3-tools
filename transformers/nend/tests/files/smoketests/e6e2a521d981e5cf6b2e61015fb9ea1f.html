      2. Shortcomings of prevailing workflows
        1. Optimized for only a single use of content
        2. Difficulty outputting multiple formats
        3. Cumbersome storage and retrieval
        4. Lead times lengthened by process itself
        5. Internal silos and resulting rework
        6. Limits to marketing and product use
      3. Product benefits available from adopting an XML workflow
        1. Shorter lead times, improving market responsiveness
        2. Readily identify, tag and monetize chunkable assets
        3. Produce multiple versions from a single source
        4. Support recombinant and reusable content
        5. Enable one-to-one or many-to-many marketing
      4. Process benefits available from adopting an XML workflow
        1. Readily support search
        2. Reduce errors and make corrections more easily
        3. Improve cross-departmental interaction (break down silos)
        4. Easily embed rights information
        5. Designing to output in XML—write once; read many
      5. Collecting data to support a business case: a planning checklist
        1. Establish and evaluate customer (end-user) requirements
        2. Assess processes across functions (handoffs)
        3. Model both current (operational) and future (strategic) benefits
        4. Solicit senior-level support for sustained change
        5. When do you “StartWithXML”?
